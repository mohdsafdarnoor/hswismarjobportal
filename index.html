AI-Powered Career Support for Master's Students

A comprehensive platform designed to support International Management students at the University of Wismar in their professional journey.

Purpose of This Platform

This platform serves as an academic resource for Master's students in International Management at the University of Wismar. It provides curated artificial intelligence tools and structured prompts to assist students in navigating the German job market, particularly in securing working student positions. The platform consolidates essential resources that support students throughout their career development process, from initial orientation to successful onboarding in professional environments.

How This Helps Master's Students

International students face unique challenges when entering the German employment landscape, including language barriers, unfamiliar application processes, and cultural differences in professional communication. This platform addresses these challenges by providing AI-assisted tools that help students understand job requirements, prepare application materials, practice interview scenarios, and adapt to workplace expectations. The structured approach guides students through each phase of the employment journey, reducing uncertainty and increasing confidence in professional interactions.

Goal of the Project

The primary objective of this academic project is to create a centralized, university-affiliated resource that bridges the gap between academic preparation and practical career readiness. By integrating artificial intelligence tools within an ethical framework, the project aims to enhance students' employability while maintaining academic integrity and professional authenticity. This initiative supports the University of Wismar's commitment to preparing graduates for successful integration into the international business environment.

⚠️ Ethical AI Usage Warning

Critical Notice for All Users:

The artificial intelligence tools provided on this platform are intended solely as support instruments and must not be used as primary content generators. Students must understand and adhere to the following principles:

  • Do NOT fully rely on AI-generated content. Artificial intelligence lacks personal context, individual experience, and authentic voice.
  • Do NOT copy and paste AI outputs directly. Employers and recruiters are trained to identify AI-generated materials and frequently reject applications that lack authenticity.
  • Companies actively screen for AI-generated CVs and cover letters. Automated detection systems and human reviewers can identify generic, non-personalized content.
  • AI is a support tool, not a replacement for personal effort. Use AI suggestions as inspiration and guidance, but all final content must reflect your unique qualifications, experiences, and professional voice.
  • Personalization is mandatory. Every document, response, and communication must be thoroughly reviewed, edited, and customized to reflect your individual background and the specific opportunity.

The University of Wismar emphasizes academic integrity and professional authenticity. Misuse of AI tools may result in missed opportunities and damage to your professional reputation.
