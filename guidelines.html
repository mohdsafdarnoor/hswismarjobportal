Guidelines

Professional and responsible use of AI for job search in Germany.

AI usage policy (simple rule)

Use AI to support your work, but make sure the final application is truthful, personalized, and consistent with your real skills.

  • Do: Use AI for structure, brainstorming, grammar, and translation.
  • Do: Add real projects, tools, results, and motivation.
  • Don’t: Submit generic AI text; recruiters can spot it quickly.
  • Don’t: Invent experience—this can cause rejection later.

Application quality checklist

  • Match the job description keywords (ATS-friendly).
  • Use 2–3 strong examples (projects, coursework, internships).
  • Show impact: numbers, outcomes, responsibilities.
  • Keep formatting clean and consistent.
  • Make the cover letter specific to the company.

Germany focus (what recruiters value)

  • Clarity and structure (simple headings, direct language).
  • Evidence of reliability (deadlines, documentation, teamwork).
  • Skills mapped to requirements (tools + methods + examples).
  • Professional tone (not too emotional, not too casual).
  • Language honesty: state your real German level.

International student work limits (important)

The student employment allowance was increased from 120/240 days to 140 full days or 280 half days per year (and there is also an alternative 20 hours/week option mentioned in official guidance). [web:382]

Always confirm what is written on your residence permit and with your employer/foreigners authority if unsure.
