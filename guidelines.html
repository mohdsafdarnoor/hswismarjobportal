Guidelines

A practical checklist for applications in Germany: structure, quality, and consistency across your CV, cover letter, and interview preparation.

Application package

  • CV: clean layout, consistent dates, role-relevant keywords.
  • Cover letter (if requested): short, specific, and proof-based.
  • Portfolio (if applicable): 3–6 strongest items, clearly described.
  • Attachments: name files clearly (e.g., Firstname_Lastname_CV.pdf).

Quality checks

  • Every claim must be true and consistent across CV, LinkedIn, and interview answers.
  • Remove filler: “hardworking, motivated, passionate” without proof.
  • Use numbers where possible: scope, time saved, users, revenue, accuracy, latency.
  • Check language: grammar, spelling, and tone before sending.

Recommended workflow

1

Target roles

Choose 1–2 role titles and build a list of companies that match your skills and constraints.

2

Tailor documents

Align CV bullets and the cover letter with the job description language and requirements.

3

Prepare interviews

Practice role questions, STAR stories, and a clear self-introduction in 60–90 seconds.

!

Use AI without losing credibility

AI can help structure and language, but applications that look fully automated are easy to spot. Keep your writing specific, human, and consistent.

  • Write in your own voice and keep the details verifiable.
  • Prefer short sentences and concrete evidence over adjectives.
  • Never claim experience you cannot explain in an interview.

Interview checklist

Before

  • Read the job description again and highlight key requirements.
  • Prepare 5 proof points (numbers, outcomes, demos, links).
  • Research the company: product, customers, and recent news.
  • Prepare 6 questions to ask the interviewer.

During

  • Answer with structure: context → action → result.
  • Be transparent about constraints and work authorization when asked.
  • If you don’t know, say so and explain how you would find the answer.
  • Take notes and confirm the next steps.

After the interview

  • Send a short follow-up message within 24 hours (thank you + 1–2 key points).
  • Track applications and outcomes so you can iterate fast.
  • Update your CV with new learnings and better proof points.
