Guidelines

Practical, Germany-focused tips for applications, interviews, and onboarding.

Important note

This portal is a student project. Always verify legal/visa rules and company requirements for your personal situation.

1) Orientation

Choose the right target

Most people lose time by applying to “everything.” Choose a clear target role family and build a focused profile.

  • Pick 2–3 job titles and 1–2 industries (example: “Marketing Intern”, “Working Student Data Analyst”).
  • Write a 2–3 line profile: who you are, what role you want, what you can do, and where you are based.
  • Collect 10–15 job descriptions and list repeated keywords (skills, tools, responsibilities).
2) Application

Make your CV easy to scan

German recruiters often prefer clarity and structure over creativity (especially for business and tech roles).

  • Use 1 page if you have limited experience; keep bullets short and measurable.
  • Mirror keywords from the job description naturally (skills/tools/methods).
  • Show impact: “Improved…”, “Reduced…”, “Built…”, “Automated…”, with numbers when possible.
3) Interview

Answer with structure (STAR)

Structured answers sound confident and professional, even if your German is not perfect.

  • Use STAR: Situation → Task → Action → Result.
  • Prepare your “Tell me about yourself” (30–45 seconds) for each category: internship, Werkstudent, full-time.
  • For international students: be direct about availability and working language.
4) Onboarding

First 2 weeks: build trust

Early trust comes from reliability, communication, and taking notes—not from pretending you know everything.

  • Clarify expectations: tasks, priorities, deadlines, and who approves what.
  • Send short updates: what you did, what’s blocked, what you’ll do next.
  • Ask questions early, but show you tried first (include what you already checked).

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Applying without tailoring: same CV for every role.
  • Overlong CVs: too much text, no measurable results.
  • Weak subject lines and emails: unclear role, no availability, no location.
  • Unclear German level: write it clearly (A2/B1/B2/C1) and show willingness to improve.

Recommended workflow (simple)

  • Pick category (Werkstudent / Internship / Thesis / Full-time) → read tools → choose a stage.
  • Use prompts to draft CV + cover letter → verify facts → submit.
  • Track applications in a simple sheet; follow up after 7–10 days if appropriate.
