These prompts are designed to help you start conversations with AI tools and organize your thoughts. They are not meant to generate final, ready-to-submit documents. Always:

  • Personalize outputs with your specific experiences and authentic voice
  • Verify information for accuracy and relevance to your situation
  • Add specific examples from your own background

Working Student / Part-Time Jobs

Prompts for finding and applying to Werkstudent positions in Germany

1. No Experience - First Werkstudent Role

Background Information to Provide:
  • Degree and specialization
  • Current semester and expected graduation
  • Relevant coursework, tools, or projects
  • Target industry or role (if known)
  • Language proficiency (German/English)
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I am a [Your Degree] student at [Your University] in Germany, currently in semester [X]. I have no formal work experience but want to apply for Werkstudent positions. My relevant coursework includes [List 3-4 courses]. I know [Tools/Software]. My German level is [A1/A2/B1/B2/C1], and my English level is [Your Level]. Please help me: 1. Identify 5-7 Werkstudent roles that match my academic profile 2. Translate my coursework into CV-ready experience bullets 3. Draft a one-page German-style CV optimized for ATS systems 4. Suggest how to address my lack of experience positively in applications 5. List common Werkstudent interview questions and how to answer them

2. Early Experience (0-2 years) - Advancing Career

Background Information to Provide:
  • Current Werkstudent role and duration
  • Key responsibilities and achievements
  • Skills developed
  • Target next role or industry
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I currently work as a Werkstudent in [Role] at [Company Type] for [Duration]. My responsibilities include [List 3-5 tasks]. I want to advance to [Target Role/Industry]. Please help me: 1. Rewrite my experience bullets to emphasize impact and results 2. Identify transferable skills I should highlight 3. Suggest how to position myself for more senior Werkstudent roles 4. Draft a professional German-style cover letter for my next application 5. Prepare answers for "Why are you leaving your current role?" interview questions

3. Career Shift - Changing Industries/Roles

Background Information to Provide:
  • Current role and industry
  • Target new industry or role
  • Transferable skills
  • Reason for the shift
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I currently work as a Werkstudent in [Current Industry/Role] but want to transition to [Target Industry/Role]. My transferable skills include [List skills]. My reason for switching is [Brief explanation]. Please help me: 1. Identify how my current experience applies to my target industry 2. Reframe my CV to emphasize relevant transferable skills 3. Address potential concerns about my career shift in my cover letter 4. Prepare compelling answers for "Why are you changing industries?" questions 5. Suggest how to network effectively in my target industry

Background Information to Provide:
  • Degree and specialization
  • University and current semester
  • Relevant coursework and academic projects
  • Tools, software, or methods you know
  • German language level (A1‚ÄìC1)
  • Confirmation that the internship is mandatory

5. Voluntary Internship (Freiwilliges Praktikum) - No Experience

  • University and study focus
  • Transferable skills or academic strengths
  • Availability and preferred duration
  • Language skills
Copy-Paste Prompt:

My German level is [Level]. Please help me: 1. Explain what German recruiters realistically expect from voluntary interns with no experience 2. Rewrite my CV to emphasize learning ability, reliability, and structure 3. Draft a short German-style cover letter (Anschreiben) for a voluntary internship 4. Suggest strong answers to common "no experience" interview questions 5. Identify industries or company types most open to voluntary interns

Provide safe, clear one-line explanations I can use in cover letters or interviews 5. Address common misconceptions employers have about international student interns

Explain which technical skills or tools I should prioritize based on these job descriptions

Suggest 5-7 strong questions I should ask the interviewer to demonstrate my seriousness

Suggest realistic backup options (research roles, startups, NGOs, smaller companies) 2. Explain how to approach professors or small companies for internship opportunities 3. Advise on managing timelines without risking my degree progression 4. Help me stay competitive during an extended search period 5. Provide strategies to expand my search effectively

Master Thesis Opportunities

Prompts for finding and managing thesis collaborations with companies

13. Finding Companies for a Master's Thesis + Job

Background Information to Provide:
  • Degree and specialization
  • University and thesis requirements
  • Preferred industry or function
  • Location preference (Germany / city)
  • Working student or part-time job status
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I am looking for a company to collaborate with for my Master's thesis in Germany, ideally combining it with a job opportunity. Here is my background: - Degree: [Your degree and specialization] - University: [Your university] - Thesis Requirements: [Duration, format, any specific requirements] - Preferred Industry: [Industries you're interested in] - Preferred Function: [e.g., marketing, operations, strategy] - Location Preference: [Cities or regions in Germany] - Current Status: [Are you currently working? Part-time? Looking for both thesis and job?] Please help me: 1. Identify types of companies in Germany open to Master's thesis collaborations 2. Suggest where Master's theses are commonly offered (corporate, SME, startup) and explain differences 3. Explain differences between corporate, SME, and startup thesis opportunities 4. List platforms, portals, and strategies to find thesis-friendly companies 5. Advise how early I should approach companies before my thesis start date

14. Convincing a Company to Allow a Thesis With a Job

Background Information to Provide:
  • Current or target company
  • Working student or part-time role details
  • Thesis duration and university requirements
  • Proposed general thesis area
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I want to propose a Master's thesis collaboration to a company where I am working (or want to work). Here is my situation: - Company: [Company name or type] - Current Role: [Your current/target position] - Thesis Duration: [e.g., 6 months] - University Requirements: [Key requirements from your university] - Proposed Thesis Area: [General topic or business challenge] - Current Status: [Are you already working here? Part-time? Looking for both thesis and job?] Please help me: 1. Draft a professional, concise email or proposal to suggest a thesis collaboration to my employer or a target company 2. Explain how to frame the thesis as a "business value" to the company, not just academic work 3. Suggest practical ways to balance working hours with thesis requirements realistically 4. Highlight common employer concerns about thesis + job combinations and how to address them proactively 5. Advise how to negotiate expectations clearly with both the company and my academic supervisor

15. Defining Business-Relevant Master Thesis Topics

Background Information to Provide:
  • Degree and specialization
  • Academic interests or strengths
  • Company industry and business challenges
  • University supervisor expectations
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I need help defining a Master's thesis topic that satisfies both my university and the company I'm working with. Here is my background: - Degree: [Your degree and specialization] - Academic Interests: [What topics interest you academically?] - Company Industry: [Company's industry/sector] - Business Challenge: [If known, describe the company's challenge or area where you could add value] - University Supervisor Expectations: [If known, what your professor expects] Please help me: 1. Suggest 3-5 thesis topics that align with my academic interests and the company's practical needs 2. Explain how to structure a topic that satisfies both academic rigor and business applicability 3. Identify common pitfalls (too academic, too operational, too narrow, too broad) and how to avoid them 4. Advise how to present and refine the topic proposal with my academic supervisor and company mentor 5. Suggest how to narrow down or focus the scope if the topic feels too broad

16. Common Problems in Thesis + Job Combinations

Background Information to Provide:
  • Current challenges or concerns
  • Thesis progress stage
  • Working hours per week
  • University and company expectations
Copy-Paste Prompt:
I am facing challenges with my thesis and job combination and need advice. Here is my situation: - Current Challenge: [Describe your problem, e.g., time management, conflicting expectations, lack of data access] - Thesis Progress: [Where are you in the thesis process?] - Working Hours: [Hours per week at your job] - University Expectations: [What your academic supervisor expects] - Company Expectations: [What your company expects] Please help me: 1. Advise how to handle situations where work is expected but thesis contribution is not recognized 2. Suggest strategies for dealing with low supervisor availability or support from the company side 3. Help me resolve conflicts between university requirements and company expectations professionally 4. Provide communication templates for addressing timeline or workload concerns with both parties 5. Suggest backup strategies if things aren't working out (e.g., switching topics, adjusting scope, finding alternative support)
