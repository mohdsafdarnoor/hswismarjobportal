FAQs

Quick answers for international students using this portal.

Before you start

This portal is a student project. Use it to speed up your job search workflow, but always verify official rules (visa/work hours, contract terms, and university requirements) for your personal situation.

1) What is this portal for?

It’s a student project that organizes practical job-search support (tools, prompts, and guidelines) for international students in Germany.

2) Which categories does it support?

The portal is structured around common student paths: part-time jobs, internships, master thesis/company collaboration, and full-time roles after graduation. [file:292]

3) Where should I start if I feel confused?

Start with Guidelines, then use AI Tools and AI Prompts to prepare your CV, cover letter, and interview answers.

4) Can I use AI to write my CV and cover letter?

Yes, but you must check every detail (dates, titles, skills, projects) and ensure the final text matches your real experience.

5) What makes a good CV for Germany?
  • Clear structure and readable formatting.
  • Role-relevant keywords from the job description.
  • Measurable outcomes (numbers, results, impact).
  • Correct dates and consistent formatting (no gaps hidden).
6) Which job portals are useful in Germany?

For broad discovery: LinkedIn and Indeed; for student part-time jobs: JobMensa; and for many listings plus recruiter attention: StepStone. [file:292]

7) How do I prepare for interviews quickly?

Practice common questions with tools like Google Interview Warmup, and structure answers using STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result). [file:292]

8) I don’t speak German well—can I still apply?

Yes, depending on the role and company. Be transparent about your German level, and focus on roles where English is accepted (many tech, international teams, and some business roles).

9) How do I track applications?

Use a simple tracker (Notion/Airtable/Spreadsheet). Track: company, role, date applied, status, follow-up date, and notes. [file:292]

10) Is the information here official?

No—this is not an official university or legal advisory service. Always confirm important decisions using official sources or advisors.

Fixing “404 not found” (common issue)
