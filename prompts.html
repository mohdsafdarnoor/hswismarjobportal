AI Prompts

Copy a prompt, paste it into ChatGPT (or another AI assistant), and replace the placeholders. Keep outputs truthful and consistent with your CV.

!

How to use prompts

  • Replace placeholders like [ROLE], [COMPANY], [SKILLS], [EXPERIENCE].
  • Ask the AI to produce “2 versions” (short + detailed), then pick one.
  • For German applications, draft in English first, then translate with DeepL and polish.

Working Student

Orientation prompt

Use this to decide what roles match your skills and schedule. 

You are my career assistant for Germany. I want a Working Student (Werkstudent) role.

My profile:
- Current degree: [DEGREE] at Hochschule Wismar
- Skills: [SKILLS]
- Past experience: [EXPERIENCE]
- Available hours/week: [HOURS]
- Preferred location: [CITY / REMOTE]
- Language level: German [LEVEL], English [LEVEL]

Task:
1) Suggest 8 role titles that fit my profile (with German + English job title versions).
2) For each role, list top 5 skills employers expect in Germany.
3) Recommend 5 keywords to search on LinkedIn/Indeed/StepStone.
4) Give a 2-week action plan (small daily tasks, 20–30 minutes/day).

Application prompt

Produces a CV bullet upgrade + short cover email. 

Act as a German recruiter for a Working Student role.

Job posting:
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

My background:
[PASTE CV BULLETS OR SUMMARY]

Write:
1) 6 improved CV bullet points (impact-first, with metrics placeholders if missing).
2) A short email to apply (120–160 words), professional and simple.
3) A checklist of missing info I should add (portfolio links, certificates, GitHub, etc.).

Rules:
- Do not invent experience.
- If a metric is missing, use [NUMBER] placeholders and tell me what to measure.

Interview prompt

Great with Google Interview Warmup practice style. 

Pretend you are the interviewer. Ask me 8 Working Student interview questions.

Context:
- Role: [ROLE]
- Company: [COMPANY]
- Job description: [PASTE]
- My CV: [PASTE]

After each answer I give:
- Give a score out of 10
- Improve my answer using STAR
- Suggest 1 follow-up question the interviewer might ask

Onboarding prompt

Helps you sound clear and reliable in a German workplace. 

You are my onboarding coach for a Working Student job in Germany.

I start on: [DATE]
Role: [ROLE]
Team: [TEAM INFO]
My responsibilities: [LIST]

Create:
1) A 30/60/90-day plan (goals, deliverables, weekly habits).
2) 5 short message templates for: asking for help, clarifying tasks, reporting progress, shift change, and being late.
3) A list of 10 questions I should ask in the first week.

Internship

Orientation prompt

You are my internship search assistant for Germany/EU.

My profile:
- Degree + semester: [DEGREE, SEMESTER]
- Target field: [FIELD]
- Skills: [SKILLS]
- Projects: [PROJECTS]
- Location preference: [CITY/REMOTE]
- Internship duration: [MONTHS]
- Start date: [DATE]

Task:
1) Propose 10 realistic internship role titles.
2) For each title: top 5 tools/skills + one mini project I can build in 7 days to prove fit.
3) Create a shortlist strategy: which 3 platforms to use first and why.

Application prompt

Act as a hiring manager. Improve my internship application pack.

Job description:
[PASTE]

My CV:
[PASTE]

Write:
1) A tailored internship cover letter (250–320 words).
2) 10 ATS keywords I should include naturally.
3) A “skills evidence” table: skill → proof (project / course / experience), no fake claims.

Interview prompt

Run an internship mock interview.

Role: [ROLE]
Company: [COMPANY]
My background: [PASTE]

Ask:
- 4 behavioral questions
- 3 project questions (deep dive)
- 2 “learning mindset” questions

Then:
- Give me a model answer outline for each
- Tell me what to prepare as supporting evidence (links, screenshots, GitHub, report samples)

Onboarding prompt

Internship goals: [GOALS]
Tools used: [TOOLS]
Team context: [TEAM]

Create:
1) A first-week checklist (meetings, access, documentation, expectations).
2) A weekly status update template (short, clear, measurable).
3) A learning plan: what to learn in week 1–4 with small outputs each week.

Master Thesis

Orientation prompt

You are my thesis topic and company-collaboration advisor.

My program: [PROGRAM]
Interests: [INTERESTS]
Strengths: [STRENGTHS]
Preferred industries: [INDUSTRIES]
Constraints (time, data access, NDA): [CONSTRAINTS]

Task:
1) Suggest 8 thesis topic ideas suitable for a company collaboration (practical + research).
2) For each topic: problem statement, objective, expected dataset, and measurable outcome.
3) Write a 120-word email to a company asking for a thesis collaboration (professional, German-style tone).

Application prompt

Help me write a Master Thesis proposal outline for a company.

Topic: [TOPIC]
Company/industry: [COMPANY / INDUSTRY]
My background: [PASTE]

Deliver:
1) A proposal outline: motivation, research question, methodology, timeline, deliverables.
2) A risk section (data access, scope creep, legal/privacy).
3) A short motivation letter (180–220 words) that matches my CV.

Interview prompt

Simulate a meeting with a professor + company supervisor.

Context:
- Topic: [TOPIC]
- Company goal: [GOAL]
- Constraints: [CONSTRAINTS]
- My plan: [PASTE]

Ask me 10 tough questions:
- 4 about feasibility and timeline
- 3 about methodology validity
- 3 about deliverables and impact

After my answers, rewrite them to sound clearer and more confident (no fake claims).

Onboarding prompt

You are my thesis execution manager.

Thesis topic: [TOPIC]
Deadline: [DATE]
Weekly time available: [HOURS]
Tools: [TOOLS]

Create:
1) A 10–12 week plan with weekly milestones.

Full-Time

Orientation prompt

You are my full-time job strategy coach for Germany/EU.

My profile:
- Target roles: [ROLES]
- Skills: [SKILLS]
- Experience: [EXPERIENCE]
- Preferred locations: [LOCATIONS]
- Visa/work authorization status: [STATUS]
- Language levels: German [LEVEL], English [LEVEL]

Create:
1) A job search positioning statement (2 versions).
2) A list of 12 target companies (mix: big + mid + startups).
3) A weekly pipeline plan: applications/week, networking messages/week, interview practice schedule.

Application prompt

Act as an ATS and recruiter. Optimize my full-time application.

Job description:
[PASTE]

My CV:
[PASTE]

Deliver:
1) A tailored “Profile/Summary” (60–80 words).
2) 8 improved bullet points aligned to the job requirements.
3) A cover letter (250–320 words) with proof-first language.
4) A list of 10 keywords missing from my CV (only if relevant).

Interview prompt

Conduct a full-time interview simulation.

Role: [ROLE]
Seniority: [ENTRY/JUNIOR/MID]
My background: [PASTE]

Ask:
- 5 behavioral questions (use STAR)
- 3 role-specific questions
- 2 questions about working in Germany (communication, teamwork, reliability)

Then:
- Provide “strong answer frameworks”
- Point out contradictions between my answers and my CV (if any)

Onboarding prompt

You are my new-job onboarding coach.

Role: [ROLE]
Company: [COMPANY]
First day: [DATE]
Team: [TEAM]

Create:
1) A 30/60/90 plan focused on measurable outcomes.
2) A weekly review template (what I delivered, blockers, next steps).
3) A relationship map: who to build trust with (manager, peers, stakeholders) and how.
